About Me

Ever since a young age, I've had a passion for technology. I've been building, coding, and tinkering ever since I can remember. I've always been interested the technical, in breaking apart things I couldn't understand to see how they work hands-on. Even when I wasn't coding, I loved building things with my hands, that I could tangibly feel take shape. Most of my expertise lies in python and linux, but I'm always looking to learn new things and expand my skillset, and I'm always looking for new projects to work on.

Work Experience

I am currently part of a research team at my university working with the Applied Meteorology faculty. The faculty here have received an NSF Eager grant to create a machine learning based model trained off the HRRR atmospheric data model to predict thunderstorms during the North American Monsoon. The model will be used to help forecasters more accurately predict and warn for thunderstorms. As a part of this project, I am using python, linux, data science, and machine learning skills.

Education

I'm currently enrolled in the Computer Engineering program at Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University with a specialization in robotics and a mathematics minor, I'm expecting to graduate in May 2026. I'm currently taking classes in python, C++, and VHDL, as well as classes in calculus and circuit design. I'm also a part of the ERAU Makerspace club, where I volunteer as a lab monitor and help students with their own projects.
