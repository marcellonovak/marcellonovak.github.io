About Me

Since I was young, I've had a passion for technology. I've been building, coding, and tinkering as long as I can remember. Even when I wasn't coding, I loved building things with my hands and seeing how they would take shape. I've always been interested in the technical aspects of my surroundings; by breaking apart things I couldn't understand to see how they worked hands-on - physical and digital. Most of my software expertise lies in Python, Linux, VHDL, and MATLAB, but I'm always looking for new skills to learn and new projects to work on.

Work Experience

I am currently part of the EAGER research project at my university working with the Applied Meteorology faculty. The faculty here have received an NSF Eager grant to create a machine learning based model trained off the HRRR atmospheric data model. This models' sole purpose is to predict thunderstorms during the North American Monsoon season. The model will be used to help forecasters more accurately predict and warn for thunderstorms. As a part of this project, I am using Python, Linux, data science, statistical analysis, and machine learning skills to create the model. I will also be creating several data conversion tools, working with databases, and creating GUIs in order to make the model more accessible.
