About Me

Ever since a young age, I've had a passion for technology. I've been building, coding, and tinkering ever since I can remember. I've always been interested the technical, in breaking apart things I couldn't understand to see how they work hands-on. Even when I wasn't coding, I loved building things with my hands, that I could tangibly feel take shape. Most of my expertise lies in python and linux, but I'm always looking to learn new things and expand my skillset, and I'm always looking for new projects to work on.

Work Experience

Starting the summer of 2023, I will be on a research team at Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University working with the Applied Meteorology faculty. The faculty have received an NSF Eager grant to create a machine learning based model trained off the HRRR atmospheric data model to predict thunderstorms during the North American Monsoon. The model will be used to help forecasters more accurately predict and warn for thunderstorms. As a part of this project, I will be using python, linux, data science, and machine learning skills.
